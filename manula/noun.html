NOUNS


    A noun is the part of speech that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Nouns can be common or proper, and
   they can act in a sentence as subject, object of the verb, and object of the preposition, among other things.

what is a noun in grammar?

Definition:
                  A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Concrete nouns name people, places, or things that you can touch, see, hear, smell, or taste. ...
                  These nouns are intangible, which means you cannot touch, see, hear, smell, or taste them using your five senses.
