Eight Parts
Of Speech

In the English language, words can be considered as the smallest elements that have distinctive meanings. Based on their use and functions, words are categorized into several types or parts of speech. This article will offer definitions and examples for the 8 major parts of speech in English grammar.

Noun Pronoun Adjective Verb Adverb Preposition Conjunction Interjection

1) NOUN

This part of a speech refers to words that are used to name persons, things, animals, places, ideas, or events. Nouns are the simplest among the 8 parts of speech, which is why they are the first ones taught to students in primary school.

Examples

  • John
  • France
  • Cat
  • Board
  • Meter
  • Waterfall
  • Fan
  • Kitchen
  • Tree
  • Class
  • Phone
  • Insect

Example Sentences

  • Tom Hanks is very versatile.
  • Dogs can be extremely cute.
  • It is my birthday.
  • I'm going home

2) PRONOUN

Any of a small set of words in a language that are used as substitutes for nouns or noun phrases and who refer to either a noun that has already been mentioned or to a noun that does not need to be named specifically.

Examples

  • I
  • you
  • It
  • We
  • Us
  • They
  • Those
  • She
  • He
  • This
  • Me
  • Him

Example Sentences

  • We are number one.
  • The largest slice is mine.
  • She is cute and I like her smile.
  • The money was really theirs for the taking.

3) ADJECTIVE

This part of a speech is used to describe, identify, or further define a noun or a pronoun. Shortly a word naming an attribute of a noun. Adjectives can specify the quality, the size, and the number of nouns or pronouns.

Examples

  • Cold
  • Beautiful
  • Sweet
  • White
  • Two
  • Fast
  • Tall
  • Young
  • Funny
  • Unique
  • Poor
  • Smooth

Example Sentences

  • The little girl has a pink hat.
  • I have three bikes and two cars.
  • That doughnut is huge!
  • She is a kind girl.

4) VERB

This is the most important part of a speech, for without a verb, a sentence would not exist. Simply put, this is a word that shows an action (physical or mental) or state of being of the subject in a sentence.

Examples

  • Am
  • Is
  • Are
  • Was
  • Were
  • Run
  • Speak
  • Create
  • Destroy
  • Go
  • Eat
  • Live

Example Sentences

  • The boy writes an essay.
  • President went to china 3 days ago.
  • We are the best team.
  • She was on the bus when i saw her.

5) ADVERB

Adverb is a word or phrase that modifies or qualifies an adjective, verb, or other adverb or a word group, expressing a relation of place, time, circumstance, manner, cause, degree, etc.

Examples

  • Then
  • Always
  • Quickly
  • Quite
  • Lazily
  • There
  • Slowly
  • Very
  • Softly
  • Too
  • Often
  • Only

Example Sentences

  • Of course, I looked everywhere!
  • Annie danced gracefully.
  • The child is very talented.
  • I have only one chance.

6) PREPOSITION

This part of a speech basically refers to words that specify location or a location in time. In other words, a word governing, and usually preceding, a noun or pronoun and expressing a relation to another word or element in the clause is a Preposition.

Examples

  • Up
  • Over
  • Against
  • By
  • For
  • Into
  • Close
  • To
  • Out of
  • At
  • On
  • About

Example Sentences

  • Don't sit on the table.
  • He hides under the bed.
  • During the game, the audience never stopped cheering for their team.
  • She arrived after dinner.

7) CONJUNCTION

A conjunction is a part of speech that is used to connect words, phrases, clauses, or sentences. Conjunctions are considered to be invariable grammar particle, and they may or may not stand between items they conjoin.

Examples

  • And
  • As
  • Because
  • But
  • Just as
  • Or
  • Neither
  • Nor
  • So
  • Whether
  • Yet
  • If

Example Sentences

  • That car is beautiful and very fast.
  • If they were older, they could go to the party.
  • They didn't go, and neither did I.

8) INTERJECTION

An exclamation. This part of a speech refers to words which express emotions and convey reactions. Since interjections are commonly used to convey strong emotions, they are usually followed by an exclamation point.

Examples

  • Wow!
  • Oh!
  • Yahoo!
  • Huh!
  • Whoops!
  • Ah!
  • Alas!
  • Hurrah!
  • Ouch!
  • Eww!
  • Bravo!
  • Aha!

Example Sentences

  • Ouch! That must have hurt.
  • Hurray, we won!
  • Hey! I passed the examination.
  • Wow! he is amazing.
