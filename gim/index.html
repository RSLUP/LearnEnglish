Nouns

What is Noun?

A noun is a word for a person, place, or thing. Everything we can see or talk about is represented by a word that names it. That "naming" word is called a noun.


Examples for Nouns

  • People: mother, father, baby, child, toddler, teenager, grandmother, student, teacher, minister,businessperson, salesclerk, woman, man
  • Animals: lion, tiger, bear, dog, cat, alligator, cricket, bird, wolf
  • Things: table, truck, book, pencil, iPad, computer, coat, boots
  • Places: city, state, country, continent, coffee shop, restaurant, park, zoo
  • Ideas: envy, love, hate, respect, patriotism, pride

Pronouns

What is Pronoun?

Pronouns replace nouns. A different pronoun is required depending on two elements: the noun being replaced and the function that noun has in the sentence. In English, pronouns only
take the gender of the noun they replace in the 3rd person singular form. The 2nd person plural pronouns are identical to the 2nd person singular pronouns except for the reflexive pronoun


Examples for Pronouns

  • Personal Pronouns: I, you, he, she, we, it, they, me, him, her, us, them
  • Possessive Pronouns: mine, yours, his, her, our, ours, its, theirs, their
  • Demonstrative Pronouns: this, these, them, those
  • Interrogative Pronouns: which, who, whom

Examples Sentences

  • The dog buried its bone under a tree.
  • Can you let Mark know that his lunch is ready?
  • I don't know how to do this math problem, so can you help me?
  • Is this pencil yours?
  • Who left the lights on in here?

Verbs

What is Verb?

A verb is a doing word that shows an action, an event or a state. A sentence may either have a main verb, a helping verb or both. In other words, a verb is a word that informs about
an action, an existence of something or an occurrence. The verb is the main word in a sentence. No sentence can be completed without a verb.


Examples for Verbs

  • Examples of verbs expressing physical action: run,talk,dance,shout,cook
  • Examples of verbs expressing mental action: think,believe,fear,want,wonder
  • Examples of linking verbs: am,is,are,was

Examples Sentences

  • I was late for school yesterday.
  • She is a wonderful singer.
  • My mom drives carefully.
  • The moon revolves round the earth.
  • Is it raining?

Adverbs


What is Adverb?

An adverb is a word or an expression that modifies a verb, adjective, determiner, clause, preposition, or sentence. Adverbs typically express
manner, place, time, frequency, degree, level of certainty, etc


Examples for Adverbs

  • Adverbs of time : never, lately, just, always, recently, during, yet, soon
  • Adverbs of place : here, there, nowhere, everywhere, out, in, above, below
  • Adverbs of manner : neatly, slowly, quickly, sadly, calmly, politely, loudly
  • Adverbs of Degree : almost, quite, nearly, too, enough, just, hardly, simply
  • Adverbs of Frequency : never, always, rarely, sometimes, normally, seldom

Examples Sentences

  • We recently bought a new car.
  • We went into the cave, and there were bats everywhere
  • The young soldier folded his clothes neatly in a pile at the end of his bunk
  • I’m so excited to see the new James Bond movie!
  • I rarely eat fast food these days.

Adjectives


What is Adjective?

An adjective is a word that modifies a noun or noun phrase or describes its referent. Its semantic role is to change information given by the noun.
Adjectives are one of the main parts of speech of the English language, although historically they were classed together with nouns.


Examples for Adjectives

  • Descriptive Adjectives :little man,green grasshopper,glass door
  • Proper Adjectives : swiss chocolates,american jeans,hydrogen gas
  • Adjective of Quantity : little juice,enough food,much money,many cars
  • Numeral Adjectives : first positiosion,six apples,two cups,seven days
  • Interrogative Adjectives : wich road,what time,whose car

Examples Sentences

  • He is a funny little man.
  • Swiss chocolates are famous all over the world.
  • ust a little juice is left in the jug.
  • Peter secured first position in his class.
  • Which road shall we take?

Interjections


What is Interjection?

An interjection is a part of speech that reveals the emotions of the speaker. An interjection is typically punctuated with an exclamation point.


Examples for Interjections

  • Short clauses may be used as interjections : How awful! , Way to go! , Oh, the humanity! , That’s terrible! ,You bet!
  • Sounds may be used as interjections : Phew! , Ugh! , Shoot! , Eek! , Whoa!

Examples Sentences

  • the freckles on my face
  • the alligator in the pond
  • a Mercedes from the car lot
  • an event in history
  • an advertisement on the radio

Preposition


What is Preposition?

In the English language, prepositions are words which connect nouns, pronouns and phrases to other words in a sentence.


Examples for Adverbs

  • Prepositions of time :ago, before, since
  • Prepositions of place :under, behind, between
  • Prepositions of movement/ Direction :up, down, over
  • Prepositions for agent, instruments, devices, machines… : by, with, on

Examples Sentences

  • He sat on the chair
  • There is some milk in the fridge.
  • She was hiding under the table.
  • The book belongs to Anthony.
  • hey were sitting by the tree.

Conjunctions


What is Conjunction?

A conjunction is a part of speech that is used to connect words, phrases, clauses, or sentences. Conjunctions are considered to be invariable grammar
particle, and they may or may not stand between items they conjoin.


Examples for Conjunctions

  • Subordinating conjunctions : either…or,not only…but,neither…nor,ust as…so,rather…than
  • Coordinating conjunction
  • Correlative conjunction

Examples Sentences

  • If they were older, they could go to the party.
  • Take your mobile with you in case you miss the bus.
  • He looks very fit in spite of his age.
  • They didn't go to the party, and neither did I.
  • Bill refuses to eat peas, nor will he touch carrots.
