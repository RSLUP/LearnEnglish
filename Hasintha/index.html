Nouns

people, places, and things (and animals)

Example: Dog, Cat, Garden, Work, Music, Town, Manila, Teacher, Bob

Uses: The sun shines

Pronouns

Replace nouns

Example: he, I, its, me, my, she, that, this, those, us, who, whom, you

Uses: John is hungry. He wants to eat

Verbs

Show action or being

Example: run, go, have, invite, laughed, listen, playing, singing, walk

Uses: The dog, and cat are running

Adjectives

Describe nouns

Example: angry, brave, healthy, little, old, red, smart, two, some, good, big, interesting

Uses: Brown dog, Fat cat, Big graden

Adverbs

describe verbs, adjectives or other adverbs

Example: badly, fully, hardly, nearly, never, quickly, silently, well, very, really, almost

Uses: Runs quickly, Eats very slowly

Articles

signal that a noun is going to follow

Example: the, a, an

Uses: The dog, The cat

Propositions

Show relationship between words in a sentence

Example: above, before, except, from, in, near, of, since, between, upon, with, to, at, after, on

Uses: I am going to my Garden

Conjunctions

Connect words, phrases, clauses or sentences

Example: and, or, but, so, after, before, unless, either, neither, because, since

Uses: I was tires so I went to sleep

Interjections

Exclamations that express strong feelings

Example: aha!, gosh!, great!, hey!, oh!, oops!, phew!, oh!, ouch!, hi!, well

Uses: Oops! I spilled the milk
